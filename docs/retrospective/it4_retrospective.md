In this iteration, we made significant improvements to our application. At this point in development, we believe we have implemented most, if not all, of our core “must-have” features, along with a few “nice-to-have” additions. The remaining work will focus on thoroughly testing, identifying bugs, resolving edge cases, and ensuring that our implementations behave as expected across different scenarios. Our goal is to make sure the system is stable, reliable, and aligned with our original design intentions.
We also improved our development workflow by addressing a key issue from the previous iteration. Last time, we delayed merging pull requests, which led to a large number of merge conflicts close to the deadline. In this iteration, we were more proactive. Most of us completed our features and submitted PRs by Sunday morning. This gave us sufficient time to review changes, resolve conflicts incrementally, and ensure that the application came together smoothly as a cohesive product.
Moving forward, we plan to continue polishing the application while integrating additional “nice-to-have” features. These include expanding user profile functionality, enabling more interaction between users, and increasing overall engagement. For example, features like “stocks wrapped” and friend groups will help create a more social and personalized experience. We will also wrap up the diagrams and documentations. These improvements will bring our application closer to a fully complete product.
